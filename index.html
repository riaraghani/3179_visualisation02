Living around the world

An overview of what it is like to live in different parts of the world

Today, more people than ever live in a country other than the one in which they were born. According to the IOM World Migration Report 2020, as of June 2019 the number of international migrants was estimated to be almost 272 million globally. With this continuous increase, it is important to explore and understand what it is like to live in different parts of the world. This webpage explores the different parameters one must consider when deciding to migrate to another country.


Quality of Life

Quality of Life refers to the level of wealth, comfort, necessities, physical and mental health and wellness, and material goods available to a particular geographic area.
As we move the year slider, we can see how the quality of life index increases showing a significant rise in the standard of living across various countries.





Health Care Index

Health Care Index is a statistical analysis of the overall quality of the health care system, including health care infrastructure; health care professionals (doctors, nursing staff, and other health workers) competencies; cost; quality medicine availability, and government readiness. It is expected that for countries with higher health care index will have a higher life expectancy. This trend is seen in the bubble chart that compares the health care index for various countries in different continents, with the average life expectancy for that country. Japan, South Korea, Taiwan are the top 3 countries with highest health care index.







Is world becoming more or less safe?

The radial chart shows the safety index for some of the most developed (and presumed to be “safe”) countries. Qatar and UAE have the highest safety index among other countries. On the other hand, countries like Australia, United Kingdom and United Staes of America (that usually have more migrations) are comparatively less safe. There are some commonalities among the safest countries in the world. For instance, safe countries tend to display high levels of wealth, social welfare, effective criminal justice systems, and education.








Happiness Index

How happy are people today? Were people happier in the past? How satisfied with their lives are people in different societies? These are some difficult questions to answer. The World Happiness report is a well-known source of cross-country data and research on which countries have the highest happiness index. From the bump chart, we can see the happiness index ranks for the top 10 countries from 2015 to 2021. Finland continues to be the country with the highest happiness index for four consecutive years. Other European countries like Denmark, Iceland, Switzerland and Netherlands are among the top 5 countries with high happiness index.

.




Unemployment Rate

Unemployment has many adverse effects, including: increased depression and other mental health problems, increased crime rates, overall lower economic productivity and consumption, lower rates of volunteerism, and erosion of skills. This can lead to a rising cost of living, which can be particularly hard on citizens in developing and/or low-income countries. The area chart displays the unemployment rates for some of the most developed countries. The 2008 recession effect can be seen on almost all countries. A clear effect of the covid 19 pandemic can also be seen around 2020-2021.







Cost of living

Cost of living indexes are meant to compare the expenses an average person can expect to incur to acquire food, shelter, transportation, energy, clothing, education, healthcare, childcare, and entertainment in different regions. A cost of living index is also used to track how much the costs of basic expenses rise over a period.Among the developed countries being considered, Switzerland has the highest cost of living followed by Iceland, Japan and Australia for the year 2021.
